Dream Jumper

A first-person liminal horror walking simulator with roguelite elements. You fall asleep. The spaces remember.

Concept

Dream Jumper follows Elias, a young man trapped in recursive dreamscapes — familiar spaces warped just past recognition. Each run generates a new configuration of liminal rooms pulled from his own memory, each holding fragments of the life he's trying to piece together.

The game sits between horror and melancholy. The threat is never a monster — it's the creeping sense that the architecture knows you.

Documents Produced
  • Game Design Document v3.0 — full systemic and narrative specification
  • Level Design Document: Silent Pool — atmospheric horror, memory as space
  • Level Design Document: Playdate — domestic uncanny, childhood distorted
  • Pitch deck for internal presentation
  • AI image prompt documentation for visual direction

"Both level design documents were praised for their original mechanical thinking — the narrative and spatial logic working as a single system rather than two parallel tracks."

Narrative Design Approach

The central design constraint was that space must carry story. Dream Jumper has no cutscenes, minimal dialogue, and no explicit exposition. Every piece of information a player receives comes through environmental detail, audio cues, or the behaviour of the space itself.

This meant designing levels as narrative instruments first, playable spaces second — then finding the intersection.
